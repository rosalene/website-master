Our Vision

Our overarching vision is to create a repository for the linkages between indigenous knowledge and the developed world. Our vision is to provide a sustainable development model that is intrinsically rooted in context--capitalizing on in situ sustainability knowledge--and bridging economic advancement in exchange for knowledge and access.

Currently we are working on implementation of this model in Ecuador.

The Achuar and Waorani people of the Ecuadorian Amazon have voiced their desire to host students and researchers interested in their culture, location, and livelihoods in exchange for infrastructure, economic sustainability and opportunities. We have established field school capacity to host students and researchers in one village, where we will test the model the summer of 2019.

Our future vision for Ecuador is to expand field school sites to half of the villages along the Napo River in Ecuador in Waronai territory. This requires infrastructure, pedagogical and human resource capacity building, and planning.

Through our global network, we invite other cultures to test the validity of our model in their context. The vision to expand to Indigenous sites in Mexico will be realized first by inviting community members to the Ecuador field site.
